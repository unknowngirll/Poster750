# Metagenomic Analysis of Dietary Impacts on Gut Microbiome

## Project Overview
This project investigates the taxonomic and functional shifts in the human gut microbiome resulting from two distinct dietary patterns: **Korean** vs. **Western** diets. Using a metagenomic approach, we processed raw sequencing data to identify microbial biomarkers and reconstruct genomic contigs.

## Study Groups
* **Korean Diet (KH):** Characterized by high fiber and fermented foods.
* **Western Diet (WH):** Characterized by higher fat and processed sugar intake.

## Analysis Pipeline
Our workflow integrated industry-standard bioinformatics tools:
1. **Preprocessing:** Quality control and adapter trimming (FastQC, MultiQC, TrimGalore).
2. **Taxonomic Profiling:** Identification of microbial species (Kraken2, Bracken).
3. **Visualization:** Interactive diversity plots (Krona, Pavian, Python).
4. **Assembly:** De novo metagenome reconstruction (FLASH, MEGAHIT, QUAST).
